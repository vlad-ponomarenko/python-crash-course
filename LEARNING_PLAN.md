# 2. Correct order of learning

## Phase 1: Pronunciation + Hiragana

Start here.

Why first:

- hiragana is used everywhere
- it unlocks proper reading of beginner materials
- it helps pronunciation

Do **not** start with kanji.

## Phase 2: Katakana

Second.

Why:

- many tourist words, menus, drinks, brands, and borrowed words use katakana
- less urgent than hiragana, but very useful in Japan

## Phase 3: Core grammar + survival phrases

Third.

You need simple sentence building early.

## Phase 4: Travel vocabulary + speaking drills

Fourth.

This is where your trip value explodes.

## Phase 5: Listening + conversation reps

Fifth.

This turns passive knowledge into usable Japanese.

## Phase 6: Very selective kanji

Last.

Only a few highly useful ones, not full kanji study.

---

# 3. The ultralearning principle

Learn in this order:

1. **Recognize**
2. **Pronounce**
3. **Understand**
4. **Produce**
5. **Use under pressure**

Many people get stuck at step 2 or 3.
Your goal is step 5.

---

# 4. Step-by-step plan

## Step 1: Master hiragana first

Time: about 5–10 days

Goal:

- instantly read all hiragana
- no hesitation on common syllables
- know long vowels, double consonants, small や/ゆ/よ

Study method:

- learn in small groups
- read aloud every day
- write some by hand, but recognition matters more than handwriting

Daily:

- 15–20 min drill
- 5 min reading aloud

Checkpoint:
You should be able to read things like:

- こんにちは
- ありがとう
- すみません
- おねがいします

without romanization.

---

## Step 2: Learn katakana

Time: 5–10 days after hiragana

Goal:

- read common katakana words slowly but correctly
- especially food, drinks, place names, loanwords

Why it matters for tourism:
You’ll see things like:

- コーヒー
- ホテル
- バス
- レストラン

Daily:

- 15–20 min drill
- read product names and signs aloud

Checkpoint:
You can sound out simple katakana without panic.

---

## Step 3: Build the “tourist grammar core”

Time: 3–4 weeks

You do **not** need full textbook mastery first. Learn only the most useful structures.

Start with:

- **A は B です**
  “X is Y.”
- **A は B じゃないです**
  “X is not Y.”
- **A は B ですか**
  question form
- **A をください**
  “Please give me A.”
- **A はどこですか**
  “Where is A?”
- **A がほしいです**
  “I want A.”
- **A があります / います**
  “There is / I have”
- **A てもいいですか**
  “May I do A?”
- **A てください**
  “Please do A.”
- **A たいです**
  “I want to do A.”
- basic past tense:
  - でした
  - ました
  - かったです

Also learn:

- particles: は, を, に, で, が, の, と, も

Do not obsess over perfect theory. Learn through examples.

---

## Step 4: Memorize the highest-value tourism phrases

Time: start immediately after basic hiragana; keep building all the way to the trip

You need phrase families, not random words.

### Group 1: Politeness

- こんにちは
- ありがとうございます
- すみません
- おねがいします
- はい
- いいえ
- わかりました
- わかりません
- もういちど おねがいします
- ゆっくり おねがいします

### Group 2: Self-introduction

- はじめまして
- ドイツから来ました
- 旅行です
- 日本語を勉強しています

### Group 3: Restaurant

- これをください
- おすすめは何ですか
- 水をお願いします
- ベジタリアンの料理はありますか
- お会計お願いします

### Group 4: Transportation

- 東京駅はどこですか
- この電車は新宿に行きますか
- 何番ホームですか
- いくらですか

### Group 5: Shopping

- これ、ありますか
- ほかのサイズはありますか
- カードは使えますか

### Group 6: Problems

- 助けてください
- 英語を話せますか
- 道に迷いました
- トイレはどこですか

Memorize these until they come out automatically.

---

## Step 5: Train with “micro-dialogues”

Time: after week 3 onward

Do not just memorize isolated phrases. Practice 2–4 line dialogues.

Example:

**Restaurant**

- すみません
- はい
- これをください
- かしこまりました

**Station**

- すみません、新宿駅はどこですか
- あちらです
- ありがとうございます

This is how you become usable fast.

---

## Step 6: Learn the most common travel vocabulary

Time: ongoing

Focus on nouns first.

### Highest priority vocab:

- station, train, bus, exit, entrance
- hotel, reservation, room, key
- water, tea, rice, meat, fish, menu
- left, right, straight, near, far
- today, tomorrow, now, morning, night
- one, two, three, how many
- ticket, price, cash, card
- bathroom, hospital, police

Also learn counters lightly:

- 一つ, 二つ, 三つ
- 一人, 二人
- one ticket, two tickets

You do not need advanced vocab for your goal.

---

## Step 7: Start speaking early

Time: as soon as you have 20–30 phrases

Use:

- shadowing
- self-talk
- VRChat or language exchange
- voice messages
- talking to yourself while walking

Practice:

- introducing yourself
- ordering food
- asking directions
- buying tickets
- checking into a hotel

This matters more than writing.

---

## Step 8: Listening training for tourist situations

Time: ongoing, 10–20 min daily

You mainly need to understand:

- yes/no
- directions
- numbers
- prices
- common service phrases
- simple follow-up questions

Train on:

- beginner Japanese listening
- travel dialogues
- slow spoken Japanese

Listen for recurring chunks, not every word.

---

## Step 9: Very selective kanji

Time: only after the basics are working

Do not sink time into full kanji study for this trip.

Learn only highly visible ones such as:

- entrance / exit
- station
- up / down
- east / west / south / north
- person
- day
- month
- yen
- big / small

Just enough to reduce stress.

# 5. Daily study plan

## Minimum effective version: 45 minutes a day

- 10 min script review
- 10 min vocab flashcards
- 10 min grammar
- 10 min speaking aloud
- 5 min listening

## Strong version: 60–75 minutes a day

- 15 min script / reading
- 15 min vocab
- 15 min grammar
- 15 min speaking / shadowing
- 10–15 min listening

Consistency beats intensity.

# 8. What will impress your parents most

Not memorizing 1,000 words.

# 9. Fastest learning loop

Use this loop every day:

1. Learn 1 phrase
2. Say it 20 times
3. Put it in a mini-dialogue
4. Use it in self-talk
5. Hear it in audio
6. Use it with a person or simulated scenario

That is ultralearning.
